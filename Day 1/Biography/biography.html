Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam






Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, full name Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, was an Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India (2002â€“2007). Known as the "People's President" and widely regarded as the "Missile Man of India," he played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development efforts. Kalam served as the 11th President of India, succeeding K.R. Narayanan. His tenure as president was marked by his connection with the common people, and he earned immense popularity among the youth for his visionary ideas and motivational speeches. He emphasized the role of youth in nation-building and was known for interacting with students across the country. After completing his term, Kalam remained active as a visiting professor and motivational speaker. He was a prolific writer, focusing on topics like science, technology, and national development.
